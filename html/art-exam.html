Asteroid Defenders

Join the league of "Asteroid Defenders" and protect the planet from relentless waves of incoming asteroids!

Objective:

Your goal is to shield the planet as long as possible from impending asteroids that threaten to destroy its protective shield.

Instructions:

1. Starting the Game:

- Click on the "Play" button to begin.

2. Player Movement:

- Move your spaceship around the canvas using either the `Arrow Keys` or `W, A, S, D` keys.

- Ensure you don't collide with the planet.

3. Shooting:

- Blast the asteroids by pressing `Spacebar` or `F`.

- You can also shoot blasts by clicking on the canvas.

- Blasts will expand outwards and destroy any asteroid they come into contact with.

4. Shield Health:

- Keep an eye on the shield's health bar at the top left.

- Each asteroid impact reduces the shield's health based on the size of the asteroid.

- Game ends when the shield's health reaches zero.

5. Timer:

- Your time is displayed at the top center.

- Survive as long as you can and try to improve your time with each playthrough.

6. End of Game:

- If the shield's health is depleted, the game ends.

- Click on "Play Again" to start a new game and challenge yourself again!

Purpose for the Player:

Embark on an action-packed adventure to defend the planet, improve reflexes, and set new survival time records. With the integration of real asteroid data from NASA, every game promises a unique challenge. Can you protect the planet and claim the title of the ultimate Asteroid Defender?

Communication to the Player about the API and Game:

Realism and Authenticity: By using the NASA's NEO API, Asteroid Defenders brings a layer of realism in its gameplay. While many games may randomly generate obstacles, my game pulls real-world data on near-Earth objects, making the threat of asteroids not just a game mechanic, but a reflection of actual space objects that NASA tracks. This can give players an appreciation or awareness of the vastness of space and the objects in space.

Dynamic Gameplay: With the game refreshing asteroid data by fetching from the NASA API, players can anticipate a dynamic gaming experience each time. The asteroids' size, speed, and other properties are based on real data, ensuring no two game sessions are entirely the same.

Enhancing or Subverting the Meaning of the API:

Enhancing:

- The game enhances the purpose of the NEO API by bringing its data to a wider audience in an interactive form. While the API's primary intent is for research purposes, the game transforms this data into an engaging activity, thereby creating more awareness of NEOs in a fun and immersive way.

- It gives players a tangible interaction with this data, turning abstract numbers and statistics into visual obstacles in the game.
