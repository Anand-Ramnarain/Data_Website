Why Solar Flares & Asteroids

Solar Flares are eruptions of intense high-energy radiation from the Sun's surface, mainly caused by magnetic activity. Did you know they can release as much energy as a billion atomic bombs in just a few minutes? If it reaches Earth effects directed towards us can disrupt satellite communication, GPS systems, and power grids. They can also enhance auroras (like the Northern Lights).

Close-Approaching Asteroids also known as Near-Earth Objects (NEOs), are asteroids whose orbits bring them close to the Earth. They are tracked because of the potential threat they pose to us. As NEOs pass by us harmlessly, there is always a risk that one could collide with our planet. Such a collision could have devastating consequences, depending on the size and speed of the asteroid.

Relationship between the two:

Both solar flares and close-approaching asteroids are phenomena that come from space and can have implications for Earth, they aren't directly related though, and either can cause the other to happen.
