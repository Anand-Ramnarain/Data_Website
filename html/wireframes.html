Wireframes

This is a slideshow, showcasing my wireframe. In this, the user can see the rough draw wireframes to how it became a blackout frame. This was important as it allowed me to have a visual blueprint of how I was going to start my website. It helped me to evaluate each element which I wanted to include whiles making sure it served a purpose on my website. Planning out the wireframes allow me to remove some doubt I had on what I wanted to include and achieve. In the wireframes, there is an orange circle on the frames, this is to represent a clickable object that will that the user to the top of the page.

I want to show my wireframes like this as it is like an evolution of Ideas. So the user is seeing the progression from low-fidelity sketches to more detailed mid-fidelity wireframes to help understand the evolution of design ideas. This was also to validate and Iterate my idea for my website as comparing the two allows me to confirm that my initial ideas from the low-fidelity phase have been accurately captured in the mid-fidelity version. It also brings clarity to my idea as I can see my details. This is because the low-fidelity wireframes are very basic and don’t include many detailed elements. By comparing them to mid-fidelity wireframes, I can easily spot where more details have been added.

Homepage

I did it like this for the user experience. The present navbar ensures that users can easily navigate to different sections of the homepage without having to scroll endlessly or go back to the top. Allowing for a smoother, more intuitive user experience. While Getting the user's engagement. By allowing users to get a snapshot of what each section is about, I am giving them a preview. If a particular section interests them, they can choose to delve deeper by navigating to that page.

By doing this if users in on the homepage and don't immediately see what they're looking for, they might leave. By providing a quick way to skim through all major sections, you're increasing the chances they'll engage more with one or more of my pages as they can see if I have what they are looking for.

This is also a form of adaptive design. This layout is also useable for both desktop and mobile designs. On mobile devices, where screen real estate is limited, it's crucial to make navigation as straightforward to the user.

Selection Pages

I did this to create a sort of spotlight Content. As I am showcasing a few recent or featured blog posts on the homepage, you're drawing attention to certain content. This should increase engagement and keep users updated on my website. I am also giving them a choice. I am giving the users the option to either view a specific post directly or see a list of all posts, which should empower them to decide how they want to engage with it. This will reduce overwhelming the user. Displaying all your posts on the homepage can be overwhelming and cluttered, especially if you have a large amount of posts. But giving a sneak peek followed by a "View All" or “Read More” provides a more pleasant experience as they can see what they want to view.

I also this this for flexibility in my design. This structure allows me to easily update my content. As I could change which posts are featured on the homepage, or add new content. The main aspect I had to consider was the descriptive previews. By including a small write-up or summary for each post, users can quickly determine which articles match their interests, increasing user engagement.
