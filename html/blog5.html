Data Visualisation, Art, and Representation

The idea is that data representation is an ever-evolving concept, which emerges from the realms of data visualisation and data art, forming powerful tools for conveying information, invoking emotion, and telling stories. It has been said there is an intricate relationship between data visualisation, data art and their forms of representation. By investigating the theories covered in class and the works of Kidd in “Theories of Representation”, as well as investigating “Artistic Data Visualization: Beyond Visual Analytics.” This essay will explore the intricate relationships between data visualisation, data art, and their strategies of representation, with a specific focus on my space-themed website using the NASA API, situating these visual mediums at the focus of information, aesthetics, and representation in the context of spaces and the universe where we aim to delve into the representation of data and the portrayal of these concepts and ideas for data space and the universe.

To understand their representations, we first need to understand their terms. The concept of data visualization can be understood as a tool that serves as a bridge between raw data for people to understand and acknowledge. This is because data visualization changes that raw data into visual forms, making information more accessible and understandable. The work of McCosker and Wilken (Kidd 2015. 30) emphasizes the potential of data visualization for knowledge generation and its role in shaping our understanding of various phenomena. This shows us that data visualization makes data more understandable for us to process and learn from.

Furthermore, we need to understand data visualisation in terms of its context. This is a crucial aspect in understanding the use of data visualizations in its representation. Lester's (Kidd 2015. Pp26-27) idea that context influences an image's interpretation is particularly relevant. Location, medium, and purpose of data visualization have an impact on its meaning. Looking at the pie charts on my website illustrate the threat level of solar flares to Earth and the number of hazardous asteroids close to Earth, these capture the connotative functions of my data visualization. As Machin and Mayr suggest, the communicative purpose of an image is not purely denotative but can be connotative, depending on the context (Kidd 2015. Pp27) In this case, the pie chart conveys a sense of the Earth's vulnerability to solar flares and asteroids, encouraging users to think about the implications of these natural phenomena. Since they are represented on a website that pertains to space and uses NASA API, it could be taken a bit more seriously than if it were on a website about ice cream.

Another aspect of data visualization also plays a role in showing and highlighting patterns and trends hidden within datasets. Paul Lester suggests that visual literacy became increasingly important in the second half of the twentieth century. This tells us that. Through visuals, data visualization offers a way of engaging and accessing data for comprehending and understanding data that is often ambiguous and open to multiple interpretations (Kidd 2015. Pp27). If we were to examine a space-themed website, it uses data visualization to explore patterns and trends related to solar flares and near-Earth asteroids using the NASA API. There is a bar graph which represents the number of solar flares in 2022, offering a straightforward yet insightful way to understand fluctuations over time. Additionally, there is an interactive line graph showing the number of close-approaching asteroids, providing real-time data that allows users to engage with the information more dynamically. Hovering over data points on this graph offers specific details about each asteroid's distance. These visualizations show ambiguity as their representations are not merely reflections of reality but are constructed entities (Viégas et al. 2007) Indicating to us that they may show specific aspects, but they can be interpreted in any way.

Since we established data visualisation and its representation, we can move into data art. Data art is rooted in data, but it goes further than traditional data visualization as it combines data with aesthetic and emotional elements. Thus, data art allows artists and creators to convey information through imaginative and captivating ways. Data art allows for data to be easily understandable in different ways other than graphs as it allows for data to be manipulated so that people can be more aware of something in the data emphasizes the data while making it understandable in a other than graphs and numbers.

Furthermore, data art also pushes the boundaries of how space is conceptualized and portrayed. This is because artists can use abstract and interactive experiences for engagement with the viewers/user. Looking at Ryoji Ikeda's "data. scan" work, as it translates data into sound and visual landscapes, allowing the user to be immersed in the cosmos and his work. Data art has a flexible nature as it allows artists to incorporate various cultural, historical and many other features into their work, highlighting the idea that representations are unstable and open for interpretation, as suggested by Sabine. (Roeser 2016)

In the context of data art, we can investigate my website’s data art to view a component of its representation. The website integrates with a form of data art in a game version known as "Asteroid Defenders." In the game, data fetched from the NASA API is transformed into a playful and engaging experience and is represented as a grey ball in the game. In the game, the asteroids are fetched from the API for their size and then drawn to come from random directions while approaching Earth at the centre, and users are tasked with preventing collisions by blasting the moving asteroids before colliding with Earth. This is a creative approach to data, as it not only provides entertainment but also fosters awareness of the potential dangers that real-life asteroids pose to our planet. Moreover, data art is used to challenge the standard mode of representation, as it allows for unconventional strategies for representing space and its work, moving beyond charts and diagrams, just like on my website.

Now that we have established both concept and their representation, we can view their relationship. Data visualization and data art intersect in the way of representing data as they are both used to make data understandable while making people aware of its data. While. They do also have a difference as data visualization objective and is mainly used for clarity and the delivery of information, while data art takes its limits to the boundaries of aesthetics and emotions, representing data more abstractly, but together, both visualisation and art can offer a broader perspective on representation allowing for data to be express and understood it different and understandable manner.

Further investigation into my website, we can provide where data visualization and data art meet and intersect. It highlights how these different aspects can complement each other and make a better understanding it their meaning through their different forms of representation. Looking at the visualisation, it combines static and interactive graphs, while providing factual information about solar flares and near-Earth asteroids through the API, thus providing users with a comprehensive understanding of these space objects. Simultaneously, my "Asteroid Defenders" game shows the potential for creativity and storytelling within an educational context.

Further, into the data representation. Representation can never be entirely neutral in context. This is because it carries elements of ideology and narrative construction (Kidd, 2015 36). The game, "Asteroid Defenders," is not only entertaining but also encourages users to consider the implications of asteroid threats in the real world. It should invoke emotion and fuel the imagination, creating a connection between the user and the data that goes further in representation. By combining these two aspects, there is an emphasis on the relationship between data visualization and data art, and how they work together and contribute to an intricate representation of narrative and data.

Therefore, after a careful examination and analysis of the intricate relationships between data visualization and data art, as well as their representations in the context of my website which uses the NASA API, it becomes evident that these two approaches to data presentation provide a unique perspective and contribute in their way to our understanding of datasets. Data visualization serves as a powerful tool for making raw data accessible and understandable, revealing patterns and trends within datasets, while data art combines data with aesthetics and emotions, pushing the boundaries of representation and encouraging imaginative engagement.

Through the exploration of the data art on my website, we see how data visualization and data art intersect and complement each other. This is because the combination of these two aspects not only provides informative content but also allows for creativity, storytelling, and emotional connections between users and the data being presented.

In conclusion, the connection between data visualization and data art emphasizes and amplifies how these strategies of representation in my website, work together to convey information, invoke emotions, and encourage meaningful engagement. As we continue to explore the vast expanse of data in our ever-evolving digital age, it is through innovative approaches that we can gain a deeper understanding of data and the world.
